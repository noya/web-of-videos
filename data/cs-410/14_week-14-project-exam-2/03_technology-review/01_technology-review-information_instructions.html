The Technology Review assignment is designed to provide students with an opportunity to go beyond the materials covered in the course lectures to explore an interesting course-related cutting-edge technology topic not covered in any lecture or a new idea about future technology. In this assignment, a student is required to write a short blog article on a chosen topic by the student. The topic of a technology review can be flexible as long as the blog article is related to the general topic of "text data retrieval and analysis" and has clear novelty, i.e., the content of the blog article is new and not covered in any existing articles. The goal is to allow the readers of your blog article to learn something new and useful that they can't learn from elsewhere. The following are some possibilities, but you may consider other kinds of topics as well.

1) A tutorial on a useful software toolkit or a comparison of multiple toolkits for processing text data or building text data applications.

2) A review of emerging new applications of text retrieval or analysis.

3) A review of new techniques for text retrieval or analysis.

4) A visionary essay about a new idea of promising future technology.

5) An analysis of limitations of existing technologies (e.g., analysis of a difficult query that does not work well on Google and a potential explanation of the speculated reasons why it didn't work well or a proposal of an idea to help improve the query).

Working in a team is allowed for finishing the Technology Review; indeed, if you choose a topic related to your course project for the Tech Review, you may also consider working together with the teammates of your project team for finishing the Tech Review. When working in a team, each team member must make substantial contributions to a review (i.e., must have written at least 1000 words). This means that a review produced by a team of 3 team members is expected to be much longer than a review produced by a single-student team. A review written by a team with more than one member must include a brief description of contribution of each individual member.

The Technology Review will be graded based on completion. Each team is required to submit a complete Technology Review at Week 14. This deadline is set to an earlier time than that of course project code submission so as to give the students an opportunity to read some relevant reviews (especially those on toolkits) before finishing their projects if they want to. Details about how to submit the review will be posted later.
